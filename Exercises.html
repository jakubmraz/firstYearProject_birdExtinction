Exercise 4

Outliers can have a significant impact on the results of a statistical analysis, including regression models. There is one outlier with nearly 60 years before extinction and possibly other outliers at the other end of the scale. Here’s how these outliers can affect the results:

  1. Influence on model parameters: Outliers can skew the estimates of the regression coefficients, resulting in biased estimates of the relationship between the independent and dependent variables. This can lead to incorrect conclusions about the strength and direction of the associations.

  2. Reduced model accuracy: Outliers can increase the overall error in the model, reducing its accuracy and predictive power. This can make it difficult to generalize the model to new data or other similar situations.

  3. Distortion of the residual distribution: Outliers can cause the residuals to deviate from normality, violating the assumptions of linear regression models. This can affect the reliability of hypothesis tests and confidence intervals derived from the model.

Despite these potential issues, there might be valid reasons to keep the outliers in the dataset:

  1. Real-world representation: If the outliers are genuine observations and not a result of data entry errors or other mistakes, they may represent important real-world phenomena that should be considered in the analysis. Removing them could lead to an incomplete or biased understanding of the underlying processes.

  2. Insight into extreme cases: Outliers can provide valuable insights into the behavior of the system under extreme conditions. By keeping them in the dataset, you can learn more about how the system responds when pushed to its limits.

  3. Robustness of the model: Including outliers in the analysis can help assess the robustness of the model. If the model can account for the presence of outliers and still provide meaningful results, it demonstrates the model’s ability to handle a wider range of situations.

In conclusion, it is essential to carefully assess the potential impact of outliers on the analysis and consider their potential benefits before deciding whether to keep them in the dataset. In some cases, it might be necessary to use robust statistical methods or other techniques to account for the presence of outliers and minimize their influence on the results.
